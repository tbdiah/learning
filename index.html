Tuberculosis Monitoring, Evaluation and Surveillance

Using a Performance-based Monitoring and Evaluation Framework to Strengthen Tuberculosis Programming


This online learning experience is designed for dedicated professionals working to end tuberculosis (TB) in their countries. It requires a basic understanding of TB monitoring and evaluation (M&E) and surveillance systems and explores how USAID's performance-based M&E framework (PBMEF) with standardized, high-quality indicators can improve the generation of quality data and support the analysis, interpretation, and use of that data to inform programming, identify gaps, and strengthen the overall system.

View Course Objectives

After completing this course, you will be able to:

  • Explain how the performance-based monitoring and evaluation framework (PBMEF) with standardized indicators can strengthen a TB program by identifying gaps and charting paths toward improving the diagnosis, care, treatment, and prevention of TB
  • Align a selection of standardized TB indicators with your countryâ€™s National Strategic Plan and/or programmatic priorities
  • Apply cascade analysis to identify operational issues that are preventing a TB program from achieving intended targets, goals and objectives, and suggest strategies to address them
  • Analyze, interpret, and synthesize TB data to develop M&E reports with relevant visuals to engage stakeholders, and inform programmatic and policy decisions
  • Create a presentation for stakeholders using TB data to recommend programmatic changes and address questions
  • Feel empowered/committed as an individual to make objective changes using PBMEF data to improve the TB M&E system in the country where you work
