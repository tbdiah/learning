Tuberculosis Monitoring, Evaluation and Surveillance

Using a Performance-based M&E Framework to Strengthen TB Programming

Time to Complete: 2.5 -3 hours Available in: English | French | Portugeuse | Russian

Despite being preventable, treatable, and curable, tuberculosis (TB) claims the lives of 1.6 million people annually (WHO, 2022) and remains the worldâ€™s leading infectious disease killer. To reach every person with tuberculosis, cure those in need of treatment, prevent new infections and progression to active TB disease, while scaling up innovations in detection, care, and treatment and fostering local ownership to sustain TB programs, we need people around the world with the capacity to rise to the urgent challenge.

This online learning experience is designed for dedicated professionals working to end TB in their countries. It provides essential information and guidance on how to effectively monitor and evaluate the performance of TB programs using a performance-based framework.

This course requires a basic understanding of TB M&E and surveillance systems and explores how a performance-based M&E framework with standardized, high-quality indicators can improve the generation of quality data and support the analysis, interpretation and use of that data to inform programming, identify gaps, and strengthen the overall system.
